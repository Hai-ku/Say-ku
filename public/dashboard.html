Rules of Haiku

"Haiku" is a traditional form of Japanese poetry consisting of three lines and 17 syllables in total. There are five syllables in the first line, seven in the second, and five in the third. The lines rarely rhyme. Here's a haiku to help you remember:

"I am first with five
Then seven in the middle--
Five again to end."

Traditional haikus usually include a personal observation, a concrete seasonal reference, and a pivot word or turning point that introduces an insight/shock of awareness, but you can write them about anything you want. Be creative!
